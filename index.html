Fact-Checking AI

Artificial Intelligence tools predict text based on patterns; they do not "know" facts. As a future media professional, your credibility depends on your ability to verify information and maintain research integrity.

Due Date Wed, Apr 29, 2026 @ 11:59 PM EDT
Weight 15% of Final Grade

The Assignment Landscape

This section breaks down the rationale behind the assignment and how it fits into your overall course evaluation. We are moving beyond simply generating content to actively investigating it.

The Rationale

AI tools are powerful, but they are not journalists. They can sound confident while being completely wrong. This assignment is not about catching AI in a mistake. It is about training your instincts as a responsible researcher. The future of media will include AI, and it is your responsibility to ensure it also includes accuracy.

Alternate Option (No AI)

If you are opposed to using LLMs, write your own 900-1000 word paper. To simulate the fact-checking aspect, write the paper first without researching, and then fact-check your own generated text against credible sources.

The Assignment Process

Follow these steps to complete the assignment. Click through the tabs to review the specific actions required for each phase of your investigation.

🤖 Phase 1: Generate the Text

  • Choose a research topic that is of interest to you or that you know well.
  • Use an LLM (like ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude).
  • Prompt the AI to generate a paper that is exactly 900-1000 words long on your chosen topic.
  • Save this raw text; it is the raw material you will be investigating.

🔎 Phase 2: The Fact-Check

  • Read through the AI-generated paper critically.
  • Isolate the strongest factual claims made by the AI (statistics, historical events, causal links).
  • Perform independent research using academic journals, recognized news organizations, or government databases to verify these claims.
  • Determine if the AI was accurate, partially accurate, entirely false, or lacking critical nuance.

📝 Phase 3: Final Deliverable

  • Extract the factual claims directly from the AI text.
  • Provide 1 highly credible source per claim.
  • Format all sources using proper APA citations.
  • Write a 2-3 sentence analysis for each claim detailing your findings (clear, college-level writing).
  • Submit your document by Wednesday, April 29, 2026.
